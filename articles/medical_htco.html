What’s a situation where I should come out to my doctor?

If you’d like to medically transition or are already medically transitioning, it’s generally a good idea to let your healthcare provider know, as it may impact your plan of care. You may also choose to let your doctor know if you’d like to be tested for certain conditions, as certain sexes as well as people with certain sexualities have a higher risk of certain diseases.

Alternatively, you may choose to let them know your pronouns and name so the office can use them correctly when you visit.

How do I know if my doctor supports LGBTQ+ individuals?

One way to check if your doctor is supportive of LGBTQ+ individuals is to call the office beforehand. You might choose not to give your name at the beginning of the call and instead ask if the practice has any LGBTQ+ patients. Alternatively, you can also check the Gay and Lesbian Medical Association’s Healthcare Provider Directory to see if you can find a doctor in your area.
