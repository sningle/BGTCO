Will I get in trouble for coming out at school?

There are many different options for coming out in an academic setting, however what you choose may vary based on your personal situation.

If you’re afraid you may get in trouble or be bullied by other students for coming out, find out what policies your school has in place to protect LGBTQ+ students. You may choose to first tell a trusted adult to help you speak to school administrators if there are specific requests you’d like to make, like having your name changed in their system or notifying your teachers of new pronouns. Additionally, if you’re not out at home, be sure to communicate to whoever you talk to if you would not like them to mention your identity to your parents.

Do my classmates need to know about my identity?

If you don’t want to come out to your classmates, you don’t have to. However, some people may choose to do so to clarify their pronouns or simply to have peace of mind in being able to speak openly about their life.

Whatever the case, coming out to your classmates is your decision that you should consider carefully. Remember, if there are people in your life who you would prefer not to be out to, choose who you come out to carefully. Some students may not be accepting of their LGBTQ+ peers, which might make coming out a more difficult process. If you would like additional support in coming out at your school, see if your school has a Gay/Straight Alliance. If not, or if you’d prefer to meet other queer people in a more anonymous way first, join a queer commnuity online.
