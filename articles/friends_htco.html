How do I know if I should come out to a friend or not?

Often it’s best to try to decipher whether a person is supportive of LGBTQ+ individuals before coming out to them. You might find this out through casual conversation, such as bringing up a gay celebrity and asking about their feelings on the person or mentioning a queer person you both know. While this may not directly reveal their feelings about queer people, you may be able to gauge their level of support to some extent.

Alternatively, you could ask them directly whether they support the LGBTQ+ community. If you’re afraid this may cause you to accidentally out yourself, you might further explain that you support the LGBTQ+ community and that you feel uncomfortable being close with people who don’t.

Is there a reason I should come out to my friends?

Unless you want to, there’s no direct need to come out to anyone. Many people choose to come out to their friends to create a support network of people who they can talk to about their identity, however if you would prefer to keep that aspect of your life private, that’s completely fine.

It’s totally up to you whether you choose to tell others about your sexuality and/or gender and it doesn’t make you a “bad friend” not to share that information if you’re not comfortable.
