When is the right time to come out to my parents?

If you’re unsure whether your parents will be accepting of your identity or not, it’s best to come out at a time when you know you will still be receiving support. Some may choose only to come out when they are financially independent and have moved out of their parents homes, while others may assume that going to college and being away from home will prevent tension between themself and their parents.

Ultimately, if you’re going to come out to your parents, ensure you’re safe first. If you are fully dependent on them and coming out goes poorly, it could create a very difficult situation. This doesn’t mean you can never come out to your parents, however it’s important to do it at a time that will allow you to do so comfortably.

What if my parents don’t believe me or tell me I’m too young to know my sexuality or gender?

While your parents may know you well, only you know your inner feelings about sexuality and gender unless you tell them directly. Sexuality and gender are fluid and can change over time, but that doesn’t make your current feelings about them any less valid.

Your identity is solely yours regardless of how others feel about it. If you’re worried about not being accepted or understood, find a safe adult who you can speak to about your feelings. Even if you don’t feel you’re able to talk to your parents about your identity, there are people who will believe you and accept you for who you are.
