Common LGBT Identities

Gay: Someone who is sexually attracted to the same gender as they are The word gay often refers specifically to gay men.

Lesbian: A woman who is attracted to other women. Some nonbinary people also identify as lesbian.

Bisexual: A person who experiences attracton to two or more genders. People who are attracted to others regardless of gender might choose the term pansexual instead.

Asexual: A person who does not experience sexual attraction. They may still desire a romantic relationship and label themselves heteroromantic, homoromantic, or biromantic. They can also be aromantic.

Transgender: Transgender people have gender identities that don't match the sex they were assigned at birth. Transgender people may or may not change their bodies or presentation to match their identites.

Nonbinary: Nonbinary people can have a gender identity other than male or female, have no gender at all, or be gender fluid. Nonbinary people might choose to use non-gender-specific terms to describe their attraction.
